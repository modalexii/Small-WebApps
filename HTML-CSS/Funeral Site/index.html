memorial service for

Bedwin Harrell Steely

June 28, 1926 - December 21, 2012

  • Sunday, April 21, 2pm at National City Christian Church in Washington, D.C.


Location & Parking

National City Christian Church is located in northwest Washington, DC, where 14th Street intersects Massachusetts Avenue at Thomas Circle. Designed by John Russell Pope, the architect behind the Jefferson Memorial, National Gallery of Art and National Archives, it is here that Presidents Johnson and Garfield chose to worship, and here that Mary Alice McKay and Bedwin (Bud) Steely wed 62 years ago.

Complimentary Parking is available at the Washington Plaza Hotel parking garage on Vermont Avenue north-east of the intersection with Thomas Circle. After parking, cross 14th Street towards the church and use the 14th Street entrance. Before you leave the church, ask for a parking voucher at the front desk of the church.

The church also has a limited number of parking spaces available in an adjacent garage. To access the garage from Thomas Circle, turn on to west-bound Highland Avenue, make a right to head north on 15th St. NW, then turn right again on N Street. Proceed down N Street and turn right into the alley just before reaching the light at N and 14th. The garage will be down a ramp on your left; pull in and buzz the front desk to gain entry. We suggest that you arrive early, as the garage is very small and may fill up.

More information, including street parking tips and public transportation routes, is available on the church's website.
